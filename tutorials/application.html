Application of Concepts

Knowing how all the concepts work and understanding example code is good and all, but what really matters is being able to apply that knowledge to a program.

Object-Oriented Programming

The best way to understand OOP is to relate it to real world scenarios or situations. This assignment, called Don't Fear the OOP, was my first introduction to object-oriented programming. In the assignment, I explain the concept of OOP in terms of a fairy tale. Many aspects of a fairy tale can be translated into object-oriented programming. Certain people, animals, or settings can be thought of as objects, and they can have behaviours and attributes, which are the most important parts of OOp.
